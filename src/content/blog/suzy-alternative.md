## Suzy overview

Suzy was founded in 2017 and has positioned itself as an end-to-end consumer insights platform. It combines quantitative surveys, qualitative research, and a proprietary audience in a single cloud-based product marketed as the "Suzy Decision Engine."

The core of the platform is its first-party consumer community of over one million screened and verified US consumers. Brands can survey this community, target specific demographics, re-target past respondents, and run qualitative sessions including in-depth interviews and focus groups through a feature called Suzy Live.

Suzy's customer base includes large consumer-facing organisations such as Walmart, DoorDash, PepsiCo, Estée Lauder, and Mondelēz International. The platform is built around the needs of insights teams and brand managers who run a high volume of research projects each year and want to consolidate them under a single managed platform.

Key strengths of Suzy include:

- A large, screened proprietary audience for US consumer research
- Integrated quant and qual in one platform
- AI-assisted analysis and reporting features
- Managed service tiers for teams that want research support
- Strong brand recognition and enterprise references

Known limitations cited in user reviews include US-centric audience coverage, pricing that can be challenging for smaller teams, and limitations in custom question programming for complex study designs.

## TestFeed overview

TestFeed is an AI-native synthetic audience research platform. Rather than connecting brands with a panel of real respondents, TestFeed builds AI personas modelled on your specific target audience using real survey data and behavioural signals. You upload what you want to test, and TestFeed returns a score, emotional reactions, audience quotes, and a clear recommendation in under an hour.

TestFeed is built for brand and insights teams that need to test more frequently without the cost and lead time associated with traditional panel recruitment or managed research programmes. It is self-serve, requires no scheduling, and has no minimum contract.

The platform supports testing of packs, ads, claims, prices, concepts, and names. Every test is benchmarked against human responses to ensure the AI panel reflects real-world reactions. The methodology is powered by SSR (Synthetic Survey Respondents), a published research method from Maier et al. (2024).

Key strengths of TestFeed include:

- AI panels built from your defined audience profile, not a generic community
- Results in under an hour, any time, with no scheduling
- Self-serve access with no annual contract required
- Always-on availability for high-frequency testing
- Clear output: score, quotes, emotional reactions, and a recommended next move

TestFeed is best suited for teams that want to test ideas quickly and frequently at the concept, pack, ad, and price level, particularly in CPG, FMCG, and direct-to-consumer categories.

### Methodology

This is the most fundamental difference between the two platforms. Suzy connects brands with real people from its proprietary community. TestFeed builds AI respondents that simulate how your defined target audience would react, drawing on real survey data and published research methodology.

For teams that need to talk to real consumers for qualitative depth or that require legally defensible primary data for regulatory or board-level decisions, Suzy's real-respondent approach is appropriate. For teams running high-frequency concept and creative testing where speed and cost are constraints, TestFeed's synthetic approach delivers fast, directional signal.

### Speed and access

Suzy is faster than a traditional research agency or panel provider. For teams used to waiting weeks for fieldwork and reporting, Suzy's same-day or next-day results are a meaningful step forward.

TestFeed is faster still. Because there is no recruitment, fieldwork, or scheduling involved, tests return in under an hour. This makes it practical to test ideas during a briefing, before a packaging review, or the morning before a creative presentation.

> "The difference is not just speed. It is the ability to run a test without planning for it. TestFeed fits into the creative process rather than sitting at the end of it."

### Pricing model

Suzy does not publish pricing publicly. Based on publicly reported data, annual licence costs for Suzy typically range from $34,000 to well over $100,000 per year depending on the scope of access, managed service level, and usage volume. It is designed for organisations that expect to run dozens of projects per year and want to consolidate research under one enterprise agreement.

TestFeed does not require an annual contract. Teams can book a demo and run a test without committing to a minimum spend. This makes it accessible for brand managers, strategists, and smaller insights teams who need high-quality audience feedback without the overhead of enterprise procurement.

Neither platform publishes a per-response pricing model. Suzy charges based on licence tier and feature access; TestFeed is priced for self-serve teams without enterprise minimum requirements.

### Audience coverage

Suzy's community is primarily US-based. User reviews on G2 and Capterra consistently note limited international coverage as a constraint for global research programmes.

TestFeed's audience panels are built from a defined audience profile rather than a fixed community, which means the model can be configured for different markets and demographics. This is particularly useful for teams operating across multiple regions or testing with niche consumer segments not well represented in standard panels.

## Who should use Suzy vs TestFeed

**Choose Suzy if you:**

- Run a high volume of research projects across quant and qual each year
- Need to access real consumers for in-depth interviews or focus groups
- Require a managed service with reporting deliverables
- Have an enterprise research budget and a procurement process in place
- Need US consumer research at scale with a screened, verified panel

**Choose TestFeed if you:**

- Need fast, directional feedback on packs, ads, claims, or concepts
- Want always-on access without scheduling or fieldwork
- Are evaluating ideas frequently and need to test early and often
- Do not have the budget or internal process for an enterprise panel contract
- Want AI-generated audience insight benchmarked against real human responses

These are not mutually exclusive tools. Some teams use TestFeed for early-stage directional testing and a platform like Suzy for final validation with real consumers. The two approaches serve different points in the research cycle. For teams choosing one primary platform, the decision comes down to whether the priority is real-respondent depth (Suzy) or speed, frequency, and self-serve access (TestFeed).
