## The Best LinkedIn Debugger

Our powerful LinkedIn Debugger helps identify and fix issues with your LinkedIn posts before you share them. The tool validates link previews, images, and metadata to ensure your content displays as intended. Using our debugger can save you from embarrassing formatting errors and broken link previews that could damage your professional credibility. It refreshes Open Graph tags to pull the latest information from your linked websites, ensuring your posts always feature current data.

## How to Inspect LinkedIn Posts

1. Enter the URL you plan to share on LinkedIn
2. Click "Inspect" to analyze how LinkedIn will display your link
3. Review the preview image, title, and description that will appear
4. Identify any errors or warnings that could affect your post
5. Make necessary adjustments to improve the preview
6. Re-inspect to confirm changes are properly displayed

The tool also checks for mobile compatibility, ensuring your posts look great across all devices where your connections might view them.

## FAQ about LinkedIn Post Inspector

### How does the LinkedIn Post Inspector work?
The tool simulates how LinkedIn processes your URLs, showing exactly how your post's preview cards will appear with images, titles, and descriptions before you publish.

### Why isn't my link preview showing the correct image?
This typically happens when the website's Open Graph tags are missing or outdated. Our LinkedIn Debugger helps refresh these tags and identifies what needs to be fixed.

### Can LinkedIn Link Inspector check posts with multiple links?
Yes, you can inspect multiple links individually to ensure each preview will display correctly when included in your LinkedIn content.

### How often should I use Post Inspector LinkedIn?
We recommend using the tool every time you share important content, especially when linking to newly published pages or recently updated websites.

### Does the LinkedIn Post Checker work for company pages?
Absolutely! The inspector works for all LinkedIn posts, whether from personal profiles, company pages, or group posts.
